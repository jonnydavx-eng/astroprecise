# Atomic notes

An **atomic note** captures exactly one idea, titled as a concept or claim you'd
later search for. Atomicity is what keeps a [[second-brain]] navigable: small,
single-purpose notes link cleanly, where sprawling pages tangle.

## Detail

When a source is ingested, it's broken into *many* atomic notes rather than one
long page. Benefits:

- **Reusable** — one idea can be linked from many contexts.
- **Linkable** — a precise title makes a precise link target.
- **Mergeable** — when a new source covers the same idea, it extends the
  existing note instead of creating a duplicate.

Rule of thumb: if a note needs the word "and" in its title, it's probably two
notes.

## Connections

- Is the structural prerequisite for [[compounding-knowledge]].
- Keeps the [[second-brain]] from degrading into an unsearchable junk drawer.
