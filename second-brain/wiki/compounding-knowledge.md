# Compounding knowledge

Knowledge in a linked note system **compounds**: each new note isn't just
additive, it can connect to every note already there, so the *value of the
collection grows faster than its size*. This is the "compounds like interest"
claim behind the [[second-brain]] idea.

## Detail

A new note's worth comes partly from its content and partly from the links it
forms. The 10th note can link to 9 others; the 1000th can link to 999. So the
connective value rises super-linearly even as you add notes one at a time.

The honest caveat the slogan skips: compounding only happens if the notes are
**linked and atomic**. Dump unstructured sources in and you get a bigger pile,
not a smarter one. The structure (see [[atomic-notes]]) is what turns volume
into value.

## Connections

- Is the core promise of the [[second-brain]].
- Only realised when notes follow [[atomic-notes]] and are cross-linked.
