Astrological Education

What Are Planetary Transits?

Transits are the ongoing movements of the planets through the zodiac and how they interact with the fixed positions of your natal chart — your personal cosmic weather forecast.

Every planet continues its orbital journey after you are born. When a transiting planet forms an angular relationship — a conjunction, trine, square, or opposition — with a planet in your natal chart, that transit activates the combined themes of both planets in your life. The result is a window of time during which certain energies become heightened, challenges arise, or new opportunities crystallise into possibility.

Outer planets — Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto — move slowly, and their transits last months or even years, often marking the most significant developmental chapters of your life. Inner planets — the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, and Mars — move quickly, triggering brief but immediate shifts in energy, mood, and circumstance.

Understanding your personal transits gives you a map of upcoming opportunities and challenges, allowing you to schedule major decisions, creative launches, and periods of necessary rest at the most auspicious moments.

⚡ Inner Planet Transits

Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars. Quick shifts in daily mood, communication, relationships, and drive. Duration: hours to a few weeks.

🪐 Jupiter & Saturn Transits

Last weeks to several months. Jupiter brings expansion and opportunity; Saturn brings tests, consolidation, and lasting achievement through effort.

🌌 Outer Planet Transits

Uranus, Neptune, Pluto — last months to 3 years. These mark the deepest transformations, generational turning points, and irreversible life-chapter changes.

🔄 Retrograde Transits

When planets appear to move backward. A natural time for revision, reflection, and completion of unfinished business rather than new forward launches.
