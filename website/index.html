What astrology actually is

Not prediction.
Pattern recognition across time.

Astrology is not fortune-telling. It is an ancient symbolic language for describing the qualities of time — the specific flavors of energy present at any given moment. Your birth chart is a snapshot of the precise positions of every planet at the exact second you arrived in the world. Not a generalized sign column — real positions computed from the same planetary theories professional astronomy is built on (VSOP87 and ELP2000), to within roughly an arcminute.

That snapshot is a map — not of your fate, but of your nature. The Sun describes your essential self and creative drive. The Moon reveals your emotional landscape and instinctive responses. The Ascendant (Rising sign) shapes how you meet the world. Each planet in each sign in each house adds a layer of nuance to an extraordinarily complex portrait. AstroPrecise renders all of it, with interpretations rooted in classical tradition.

Transits show where the current sky connects to your natal chart — which natal planets are being activated right now, and what themes those activations historically correlate with. Numerology adds a complementary layer: the mathematical resonance of your birth date and name, compressed into archetypal numbers that describe your soul's purpose, expression, and the rhythm of your personal years.

Good to know

Questions, answered honestly

Is it really free?

Your birth chart, daily horoscope, compatibility, transits and the Instrument are all free to use — no account, no sign-up. The one thing you can pay for is the full personalised Deep Reading: a one-time PDF export drawn from your exact chart. It's never a subscription.

What's in the paid Deep Reading?

A long-form written interpretation of your whole chart — every placement, your tightest aspects, your dominant element and the houses they fall in — drawn line by line from the chart you just cast, and set as a PDF that's yours to keep.

Is my birth data private?

Yes. Your chart is computed entirely in your browser — your birth moment is never sent to any server, and there's no account or tracking. The only outbound calls are public sky-data feeds and a place-name lookup (the typed place only, never your birth time).

How accurate is it?

Positions come from real astronomy — VSOP87 for the planets, ELP2000 for the Moon — accurate to roughly an arcminute for dates between 1800 and 2200 CE. Every figure on the site is labelled measured or computed, never invented. See exactly how →
